Glossary

a

ANAEROBIC DIGESTION

Anaerobic digestion is a biological process where microorganisms break down organic matter without oxygen, producing biogas (mainly methane and carbon dioxide). This renewable energy reduces waste and greenhouse gases.

AGFLATION

The increase in food price that occurs as a result of increased demand on food for human consumption and energy.

b

BIODIESEL

Biodiesel is a renewable fuel made from vegetable oils, animal fats, or recycled grease. It's biodegradable and reduces greenhouse gases, making it an eco-friendly alternative to traditional diesel fuel.

e

ECO FOOTPRINT

An eco footprint measures the environmental impact of human activities, calculating the resources consumed and waste produced. It reflects how much of Earth's resources are needed to sustain a lifestyle.

EMISSION TRADING

Emission trading is a market-based system where companies trade pollution allowances to meet environmental goals and reduce greenhouse gases.

ETHICAL CONSUMERISM

Ethical consumerism involves making purchasing decisions based on moral values, such as environmental sustainability, fair trade, animal welfare, and labor rights, promoting responsible practices and reducing negative impacts on society and the planet.

d

DECENTRALISED ENERGY

Decentralised energy refers to energy generated close to its point of use, often from renewable sources, reducing transmission losses, enhancing energy security, and empowering communities with sustainable, localized power solutions.

T

TRUNKING

Trunking is a networking technique that combines multiple network lines into one channel, optimizing bandwidth and efficiency. Common in telecommunications, it allows multiple users to share a single communication path.
